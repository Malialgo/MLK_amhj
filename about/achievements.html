Martin Luther King, Jr.'s Achievements

MLK has done many things that have benefited this world tremendously, and during less than the 13 years of his leadership of the American Civil Rights Movement, African Americans attained progress towards racial equality.

  1. In 1955, King was hired as a a spokesman for the Montgomery Bus Boycott, a campaign by the African American population of Montgomery, AL to demand integration of the city bus lines. After #81 days of participation from the black community, the U.S. Supreme court decided that racial segregation in transportation was unconstitutional.
  2. In 1957, King was elected president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference, an organization designed to provide new leadership for the now burgeoning civil rights movement.
  3. In 1963, King led a coalition of many civil rights groups in a nonviolent campaign aimed at Birmingham, AL, which at the time was known as the "most segregated city in America". Witnessing the brutality of the city's police towards the people led to a national outrage resulting in a push for unprecedented civil rights legislation. It was during this campaign that Dr. King drafted the Letter from a Birmingham Jail, the manifesto of Dr. King's philosophy and tactics.
  4. In 1964, at 35 years old, King became the youngest person to win the Nobel Peace Prize.
  5. In 1965, Congress went on to pass the Voting Rights Act, a set of laws that eliminated the remaining barriers to voting for African-Americans, who in some locales had been almost completely disenfranchised. This legislation resulted directly from the Selma to Montgomery, AL march for Voting Rights lead by Dr. King.
